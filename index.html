Sustainability Through the Ages

Milestones that shaped Intel's technology leadership and sustainability goals.

1968: Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of innovation.

1971: First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the first commercial microprocessor, igniting the modern computing era.

1978: 8086 Processor

The 8086 launches the x86 architecture that powers PCs and servers today.

1985: 386 Processor

Intel introduces 32-bit architecture, unlocking new performance and multitasking for personal computers.

2006: Peak GHG Emissions

Intel invests in abatement and renewable energy to reverse emissions trends.

2020: RISE Strategy

Intel launches RISE goals across climate, water, and waste stewardship.

2022: Net-Zero by 2040

Intel commits to net-zero Scope 1 and 2 emissions across global operations.

2023: Renewable Electricity

The company reaches 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide.

2024: Sustainability Summit

Intel convenes suppliers and leaders to shape sustainable manufacturing.
